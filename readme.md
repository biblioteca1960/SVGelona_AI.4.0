## Key Concepts

### Fundamental Tetrahedron
- Geometric boundary condition
- All critical trajectories pass through this tetrahedron
- Basis for connecting geometric properties with the critical line of the Riemann Hypothesis

### Icosahedra and Angular Defects
- Each icosahedron vertex generates families of critical points along fractal trajectories
- Angular defect measures misalignment between rotated icosahedra
- Vector `abc` transforms into `t3-` along critical lines forming spirals

### ChronNet (Temporal Geometric Network)
- Encodes temporal depth and relationships between trajectories
- Enables navigation of "preconscious thought" paths

### Möbius 8-Symmetry Loop
- Applies 8 symmetry rotations to icosahedra  
- Generates global holonomy and alignment patterns  
- Encapsulates the **Global Icosahedral structure**

### Tesseracts
- **FixedTesseract:** Invariant axes for self-regulation  
- **FloatingTesseract:** Dynamic vectors aligned to fixed axes  
- Auto-regulation ensures all floating tesseracts converge along critical planes

### Emergent Geometric Language
- Symbols (`Δ, Λ, Ω, Φ, Ψ, Χ`) represent geometric/conceptual states  
- Grammar emerges from transitions between nodes in ChronNet  
- Enables mapping from trajectories to symbolic sentences

### Geometric Search & Preconscious Exploration
- Search trajectories by initial vertex, angle, or conceptual association  
- Explore coherent thought paths in the temporal network
